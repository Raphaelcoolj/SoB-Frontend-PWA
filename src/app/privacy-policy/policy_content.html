Last updated June 20, 2026

1. WHAT INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT?

Personal information you disclose to us
In Short: We collect personal information that you provide to us.

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register on the Services, express an interest in obtaining information about us or our products and Services, when you participate in activities on the Services, or otherwise when you contact us.

The personal information we collect may include:
  • Names
  • Email addresses
  • Usernames and passwords
  • Date of birth (used for safety checks and sensitive content controls)
  • Priority fields/interests selected during onboarding
  • Profile pictures (avatars) and bio description
  • Posts, articles, comments, bookmarks, and engagement records
  • Follow relationships, block lists, and notification configurations
  • Google OAuth profile data (if you sign in using Google)
Sensitive Information. We do not process sensitive personal information.

Information automatically collected
In Short: Some information — such as your Internet Protocol (IP) address and/or browser and device characteristics — is collected automatically when you visit our Services.

We automatically collect certain information when you visit, use, or navigate the Services. This information does not reveal your specific identity but may include device and usage information, such as your IP address, browser and device characteristics, operating system, language preferences, referring URLs, device name, country, location, information about how and when you use our Services, and other technical information. This is needed to maintain the security and operation of our Services, and for our internal analytics and reporting.

The information we collect automatically includes:
  • Log and Usage Data: Diagnostic, usage, and performance logs generated by our servers.
  • Device Data: Device model, manufacturer, browser version, and platform configuration.
  • Push Subscription Tokens: Web push endpoint credentials if notifications are enabled.

2. HOW DO WE PROCESS YOUR INFORMATION?
In Short: We process your information to provide, improve, and administer our Services, communicate with you, for security and fraud prevention, and to comply with law.

We process your personal information for a variety of reasons, including:
  • To facilitate account creation and authentication: To verify your credentials and register your account.
  • To deliver and facilitate delivery of Services: To process and display your posts, articles, comments, bookmarks, and user profile page.
  • To personalize content (FYF): To generate and score a custom For-You-Feed containing topics and articles matching your priority fields.
  • To enforce blocking, unfollowing, and account privacy options: To restrict posts and profile accessibility depending on blocking decisions and private/public toggles.
  • To send notifications: To distribute email digests, real-time push alerts, or web socket notices regarding likes, comments, and new followers.
  • To protect our Services: For security monitoring, abuse detection, and fraud prevention.

3. WHAT LEGAL BASES DO WE RELY ON TO PROCESS YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION?
We process your personal information only when we believe it is necessary and we have a valid legal reason (i.e., legal basis) to do so under applicable law, such as:
  • Consent: You have given us clear permission to process your information for a specific purpose.
  • Performance of a Contract: Processing is required to deliver the core functionalities of the SoB platform.
  • Legitimate Interests: To improve our application, analyze engagement, and secure the platform.
  • Legal Obligations: To comply with safety regulations, age requirements (e.g. COPPA), and court orders.

4. WHEN AND WITH WHOM DO WE SHARE YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION?
We may share your personal data with third-party service providers who assist us in operating our platform, including:
  • Cloud Storage Providers: Cloudinary (for storing and serving user avatars and images).
  • Video Infrastructure: Mux (for uploading, converting, and playing video attachments).
  • Email Services: Resend / Brevo (for sending verification pins, password resets, and digest summaries).
  • Database Services: MongoDB Atlas (for storing app records and relationships securely).

5. HOW DO WE HANDLE YOUR SOCIAL LOGINS?
If you choose to sign up or log in to the Services using a Google account, we access public profile information, including your full name, email address, and avatar URL. We use this information to populate your SoB profile and verify your email. You can revoke Google OAuth permissions at any time through your Google account settings.

6. IS YOUR INFORMATION TRANSFERRED INTERNATIONALLY?
Our servers and third-party providers are located globally. Your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in jurisdictions other than your home country. We implement appropriate safeguards to protect your personal data in accordance with this Privacy Notice.

7. HOW LONG DO WE KEEP YOUR INFORMATION?
We keep your personal information for as long as you maintain an active account on SoB. If you request account deletion, we immediately trigger a full cascade delete: all your posts (including Cloudinary/Mux assets), comments, follow associations, block list histories, notifications, and profile details are purged from our live database.

8. HOW DO WE KEEP YOUR INFORMATION SAFE?
We employ industry-standard technical and organizational security measures, including hashing passwords (using bcrypt), applying JSON Web Tokens (JWT) for session authorization, and running databases behind secure access tokens. However, no transmission over the internet can be 100% secure.

9. WHAT ARE YOUR PRIVACY RIGHTS?
Depending on your region, you have rights such as accessing your data, updating outdated information, limiting processing, or completely deleting your profile. You can exercise these directly within your profile settings page or by emailing our team.

10. CONTROLS FOR DO-NOT-TRACK FEATURES
Most web browsers include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature signaling your privacy preference not to have data about your online activities monitored. Since there is no uniform standard for DNT signal recognition, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals.

11. DO UNITED STATES RESIDENTS HAVE SPECIFIC PRIVACY RIGHTS?
Yes. Residents of states like California (under CCPA/CPRA), Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and Utah have rights to access, edit, delete, or obtain portability of their personal data, as well as opt out of targeted advertisements. We do not sell or lease your personal information.

12. DO OTHER REGIONS HAVE SPECIFIC PRIVACY RIGHTS?
Yes. Under the GDPR (European General Data Protection Regulation) and UK GDPR, visitors from the EEA and United Kingdom have rights of data portability, restriction of processing, objection to profiling, and the right to be forgotten.

Yes, we may update this Privacy Notice from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by an updated "Last revised" date and will be effective as soon as it is accessible. We encourage you to review this notice frequently.
